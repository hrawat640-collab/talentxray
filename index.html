How to use TalentXRay

A step-by-step guide to finding anyone on the internet using Google X-Ray search — no Boolean knowledge required.

1

Pick your platform

LinkedIn is the default and best for most roles. For engineers, also try GitHub. For designers, Behance and Dribbble. For data scientists, Kaggle. You can select multiple — they'll be searched together in one query.

When LinkedIn is selected, choose the correct region. India is the best-indexed globally. If hiring in the UK or Canada, switch to those subdomains for far better results.

2

Enter the role

Type the primary job title as candidates write it on their profiles. Add alternative titles — if looking for a "Product Manager", also add "PM" and "Product Lead" since people use different titles for the same role.

Select seniority to narrow the pool. Experience range is available for non-LinkedIn platforms where profiles contain that text.

3

Add skills — keep it lean

Must-have (AND) — only the 2–3 non-negotiables. Every extra AND halves your result pool. More than 3 on LinkedIn = zero results.

Nice-to-have (OR) — everything else goes here. The candidate only needs one of these to appear.

✅ Good: Must-have: Python, AWS · Nice-to-have: Docker, Kubernetes, Terraform
❌ Bad: Must-have: Python, AWS, Docker, Kubernetes, Terraform, GCP, React
4

Location, companies and exclusions

Location narrows to a city or country. Target companies finds candidates with all those companies in their profile history — great for specific pedigree combinations.

Exclude keywords remove noise — "Recruiter", "Student", "Intern", "Fresher" filters out ~30% of irrelevant profiles instantly.

5

Open in Google, then use an extension

Click Open in Google. You'll see up to 100 real profile pages. To extract emails and phone numbers, install a Chrome extension like Apollo.io, ContactOut, or Hunter.io — these run on the LinkedIn profile and surface contact info automatically.

Only getting 10 results? Click Variations to generate 5 alternative queries. Each returns a fresh batch — together that's 50–200+ unique profiles per session.

Frequently asked questions
Why am I only getting 10 results on LinkedIn?
Google caps X-Ray searches at ~10–30 results per query — this is a Google and LinkedIn limitation, not a tool issue. Use the Variations button to generate 5 different versions of your query. Run each one to get a fresh batch. Together they give you 50–200+ unique candidates from one search session.
Why does US LinkedIn return almost no results?
LinkedIn has progressively blocked Google from indexing US profiles to push companies toward LinkedIn Recruiter (their paid product). For US hiring, GitHub works better. India, UK, and Canada subdomains are still well-indexed.
How do I get email addresses from the profiles?
TalentXRay finds the profiles — contact extraction requires a separate Chrome extension. The most popular options are Apollo.io, ContactOut, and Hunter.io. Install one, open a LinkedIn profile from your results, and the extension surfaces the email and phone number automatically.
Why do my searches return zero results?
Too many AND conditions is the most common cause. Check your query strength meter — if it shows red, remove 2–3 must-have skills and move them to nice-to-have. Also try removing the location field — many profiles don't explicitly state their city.
Can I use this for non-tech roles?
Absolutely. TalentXRay works for any role with a professional presence online. Finance, HR, Operations, Marketing, Sales — all work well on LinkedIn. The skills suggestion library includes HR & People, Finance & Ops, and Design categories specifically for non-technical searches.
